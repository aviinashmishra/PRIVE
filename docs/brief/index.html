A blockchain carbon exchange, specified for the market and the planet.

Prive Exchange trades tokenised carbon credits with the reliability of a crypto exchange and the integrity of a public registry. This dossier turns the product vision into build-ready engineering: architecture, data models, API and contract interfaces, a threat model, and a costed Phase-1 plan.

Technical planning artifacts — nine specifications, three supporting deliverables. No application code yet; this is the source of truth a team builds against.

Test one

Can a trader trust the market?

Exchange-grade matching, custody, and surveillance: a Rust order book at <10 ms, an append-only double-entry ledger, MPC custody with 95%+ cold storage, and real-time abuse detection.

Test two

Can the planet trust the credit?

One token equals one registry-retired tonne — enforced in the contract. Full on-chain lifecycle from issuance to retirement, provenance anchored to IPFS, auditable by anyone in the public explorer.

The central decision

A hybrid exchange: fast where it must be, permanent where it counts

Order matching runs off-chain for millisecond latency; issuance, settlement, and retirement land on-chain for auditability. A continuous reconciliation job proves the two always agree.

Off-chain · source of truth for balances

Latency & matching

Where a trader's experience is decided.

  • Rust order book, price-time priority, <10 ms match
  • Event-sourced on Kafka — deterministic replay on restart
  • Double-entry ledger with holds placed before every match
  • Live order book, candles, and depth over WebSocket
Merkle-batched settlement
On-chain · source of truth for existence

Issuance & finality

Where the planet's trust is decided.

  • ERC-1155 credit batches — one token id per project-vintage
  • Mint blocked unless the registry tonne was retired at source
  • Retirement burns the credit and issues a certificate NFT
  • Every credit's lifecycle public in the Transparency Explorer

Phase 1 boundary

What ships first — and what is deliberately deferred

The MVP is specified in build-ready detail. Later phases are seamed at the interface level so Phase 1 never paints us into a corner.

In Phase 1 (MVP)

  • Dual buyer onboarding — individual and corporate, tiered KYC / KYB
  • Admin-minted tokenisation of pre-verified credits
  • Spot trading on 3–5 pairs with live charts and order book
  • Basic mining — verified steps and referrals
  • On-chain retirement with certificate NFT and PDF
  • Admin core and the public Transparency Explorer

Deferred to Phase 2 & 3

  • Self-serve seller portal and verification pipeline
  • Auctions, storefronts, and the corporate OTC desk
  • Advanced order types — OCO, iceberg, TWAP, recurring buy
  • MRV oracles and streaming credit issuance
  • Fiat on / off-ramp and mobile apps
  • Forward contracts, hybrid AMM, governance token
Delivery plan

Eight two-week sprints to a demonstrable MVP

Sequenced by dependency, not optimism. Mainnet launch is gated by two independent audits and a bug bounty — an audit-gated date, never a calendar one.

S0

Foundations

Monorepo, infrastructure, CI/CD, contracts on the Amoy testnet — and the four open decisions resolved.

S1

Identity & KYC

Dual-track onboarding, tiered verification, RBAC, and organisation membership.

S2

Ledger & wallet

Double-entry engine, holds, deposits and withdrawals with allowlist and 24-hour new-address delay.

S3

Matching engine

In-memory order book, Kafka event log, market configuration, deterministic crash-replay.

S4

Trading API & market data

Order lifecycle, portfolio, OHLCV candles, and sub-100 ms WebSocket fan-out.

S5

Tokenisation, settlement & retirement

Admin mint, Merkle-batched settlement, certificate NFTs, and the reconciliation safety net.

S6

Mining & admin core

Proof-of-green-action with anti-fraud, and the hardware-key-gated mission-control console.

S7

Explorer & hardening

Transparency Explorer, i18n and accessibility, load test, pen test, and the security game day.

M5

Mainnet — audit-gated

Two independent contract audits and a public bug bounty complete, plus legal sign-off per jurisdiction. Not a fixed sprint by design.

Needs a human

Four decisions that block Sprint 0

These are product and legal calls, not engineering ones. Each reshapes a specific module — resolving them early is the cheapest they will ever be. They are flagged in every affected document, not buried.
Q1

Launch jurisdiction(s)

Determines KYC tiers, geo-gating, and whether a tokenised credit is a commodity, a security, or an environmental instrument.

Q2

Fiat at MVP, or not

Crypto-settled only for Phase 1, or a payment on-ramp on day one — it changes the ledger, the payments API, and scope.

Q3

Custody model

Managed Fireblocks-class MPC for speed, or self-hosted MPC for control — it drives mint authority and key management.

Q4

Registry integration reality

Admin-attested registry serials at MVP with automated Verra / Gold Standard cross-check in Phase 2 — or block on API access now.
